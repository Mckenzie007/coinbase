What is Decentralization?

Understanding the core principle behind blockchain technology.

Decentralization is the process of distributing power away from a central authority. In blockchain technology, this means that no single entity controls the entire network. Instead, control is distributed among many participants, making the system more democratic and resistant to censorship.

Coinbase leverages decentralized technologies to provide you with access to a global financial system that is open to everyone, without needing to rely on traditional financial institutions.

Key benefits of decentralization include:

  • Increased security and resistance to attacks
  • Greater transparency of transactions
  • Reduced reliance on intermediaries
  • Global accessibility

How to Create a Coinbase Wallet

Step-by-step guide to setting up your cryptocurrency wallet.

Creating a Coinbase Wallet is your first step to securely storing cryptocurrencies and exploring decentralized applications. Here's how to get started:

  1. Download the Coinbase Wallet app from the App Store or Google Play
  2. Open the app and tap "Create new wallet"
  3. Read and accept the terms of service
  4. Create a secure username that will be your Wallet username
  5. Back up your recovery phrase in a secure location
  6. Confirm your recovery phrase
  7. Set up your preferred security method (biometrics or PIN)

Your Coinbase Wallet is now ready! You can receive cryptocurrency by sharing your wallet address, or explore the world of decentralized finance through the built-in dapp browser.

What is a Token on Coinbase?

Understanding digital assets on the Coinbase platform.

Tokens are digital assets built on existing blockchains. On Coinbase, you can trade various types of tokens:

Cryptocurrencies: Digital currencies like Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH) that operate on their own blockchains.

ERC-20 Tokens: Tokens built on the Ethereum blockchain that represent assets or utilities. Examples include USDC, DAI, and UNI.

Stablecoins: Tokens pegged to stable assets like the US dollar to minimize volatility. Examples include USDC and DAI.

Utility Tokens: Provide access to a product or service within a specific ecosystem.

Governance Tokens: Allow holders to vote on decisions about a protocol's future.

Coinbase carefully evaluates each token before listing to ensure it meets our security, compliance, and technical standards. You can view all supported tokens in your Coinbase account's "Assets" section.

Understanding Cryptocurrency Transaction Fees

Learn about the fees associated with crypto transactions.

Cryptocurrency transaction fees vary based on several factors:

Network Fees: Paid to miners or validators for processing transactions on the blockchain. These fluctuate based on network congestion.

Coinbase Fees: Our fee structure includes a spread of about 0.50% for cryptocurrency conversions and an additional Coinbase Fee based on payment method.

Factors affecting fees:

  • Payment method (bank transfer, debit card, etc.)
  • Order size
  • Market conditions
  • Location and jurisdiction

To minimize fees, consider using ACH bank transfers for deposits and limit transactions during periods of high network congestion. Always review fees before confirming any transaction.

Securing Your Coinbase Account

Best practices to protect your cryptocurrency investments.

Protecting your Coinbase account is crucial for safeguarding your cryptocurrency assets. Follow these security best practices:

  1. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use an authenticator app for the highest level of security
  2. Use a Strong Password: Create a unique password that you don't use elsewhere
  3. Verify Email Notifications: Enable notifications for all account activity
  4. Beware of Phishing: Always check URLs and never share your credentials
  5. Use Whitelisting: Enable address whitelisting for withdrawals
  6. Secure Your Recovery Phrase: Store it offline in multiple secure locations
  7. Keep Software Updated: Ensure your OS and antivirus are current

Coinbase also provides additional security features like the Vault for long-term storage and delayed withdrawals for added protection.

How to Verify Your Identity

Complete the verification process to access all features.

Identity verification is required to comply with financial regulations and to protect your account. Here's how to complete the process:

  1. Sign in to your Coinbase account
  2. Go to Settings > Personal Information
  3. Click "Verify Identity"
  4. Select your document type (driver's license, passport, or ID card)
  5. Upload clear photos of your document (front and back if applicable)
  6. Take a selfie for facial verification
  7. Submit and wait for verification (usually takes 1-5 minutes)

Tips for successful verification:

  • Use a well-lit area without glare
  • Ensure all document details are visible
  • Remove any covers from your ID
  • Use a plain background for your selfie
  • Make sure your face matches your ID photo

If you encounter issues, our support team can assist you with the verification process.
